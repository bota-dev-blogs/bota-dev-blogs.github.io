## What is Otter.ai?

[Otter.ai](https://otter.ai) is a popular app for transcribing meetings. It works well for virtual video conferencing meetings and also supports in-person meetings.

For virtual meetings, Otter sends a bot to join the user's meeting to record and transcribe. For in-person meetings, Otter relies on a mobile app to record the conversation.

If you're building a transcription or meeting intelligence product, you might consider enabling in-person capture similar to Otter, especially as hybrid work becomes the norm.

However, relying on phones or laptops to capture offline meetings introduces limitations in audio quality, reliability, and scalability.

## Does Otter Provide Dedicated Hardware for In-Person Meetings?

No. Otter does not offer dedicated meeting room hardware.

For virtual meetings, bots work well because audio streams are already digitized. But physical meeting rooms require microphones, edge processing, connectivity, and secure syncing infrastructure.

Without purpose-built hardware, in-person recording depends on:

- A user remembering to open an app
- A phone placed in the middle of the table
- Consumer-grade microphones
- Manual workflows

For teams building transcription products, this creates a gap between virtual and physical meeting capture.

## Ways to Support In-Person Meeting Recording

If you're building a transcription platform and want to support offline meetings, there are a few options:

### 1. Rely on Mobile Devices

You can ask users to record meetings using their phones or laptops.

**Benefits**

- Easy to implement
- No additional hardware required

**Downsides**

- Inconsistent audio quality
- Poor speaker capture in larger rooms
- Battery limitations
- Manual user actions required
- Not ideal for enterprise deployments

This approach works for small meetings but does not scale well for conference rooms.

### 2. Build Your Own Hardware

Another option is to design and manufacture your own dedicated recording devices.

This would allow you to:

- Optimize microphone arrays
- Control device firmware
- Manage security and syncing
- Deliver a seamless in-room experience

However, building hardware is complex. It requires:

- Industrial design
- Supply chain management
- Firmware development
- Certification and compliance
- Ongoing device management infrastructure

For most software companies, this adds years of development and significant operational overhead.

### 3. Integrate with a Hardware + API Platform

Instead of building hardware from scratch, you can integrate with an existing infrastructure provider.

With Bota, transcription platforms can:

- Deploy physical recording devices in meeting rooms

- Securely sync recordings to the cloud
- Access audio via API

This allows software companies to add offline meeting support without becoming a hardware company.
