# Pipeline 1 Motion Grammar

Pipeline 1 is an arrow-free explainer. Motion should clarify hierarchy while
leaving every card readable in the first frame.

| Layout family | Visual rhythm | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| `row`, `grid`, `mosaic`, `bento` | staggered card pulse plus a restrained scan | peers, evidence, or one thesis with facets |
| `timeline`, `flow-board`, `route-map`, `staircase` | a rail carries two small moving pulses | order, handoffs, recovery, or progression |
| `radial`, `orbit`, `system-board` | center pulse plus orbit or spoke activity | one primitive coordinates several systems |
| `signal-board` | a waveform rail with alternating attachment points | audio, streams, artifacts, or transformations |
| `console`, `dashboard`, `split-focus` | one strong block and a live status column | a primary result with operational constraints |
| `checklist`, `stacked`, `bands` | sequential marker or horizontal scan | rules, layers, or tests |
| `compare`, `lanes`, `matrix`, `triptych` | stable separation with alternating emphasis | alternatives, dimensions, or parallel views |
| `failure-focus`, `spotlight` | one pulse ring around the decision or risk | one dominant failure, risk, or result |

## Density

- Use 1-4 cards. A card is a claim, not a paragraph.
- Keep visible titles concrete and captions short enough to fit without a tiny
  font. Never add page numbers or internal editorial labels to create texture.
- Use one rail, scan, or pulse system per page. The renderer adds the motion;
  the storyboard should not encode frame-by-frame coordinates.
- Prefer `illustration`-like concrete icons in the first or primary card and
  smaller `card` content for supporting evidence. Use icons that name the
  object: `wearable`, `bluetooth`, `waveform`, `api`, `storage`, `asr`, or
  `webhook` are stronger than a generic abstraction.
